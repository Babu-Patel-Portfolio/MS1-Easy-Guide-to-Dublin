Travel

Dublin city is served by a number of travel options providing good transport links within the city and to the rest of the country, though not always good value. Again it's worth checking out seasonal offers, particularly when travelling by train during the summer months.

Bus

Dublin Bus is the main provider of a regular public bus service within the city. A reduced service operates at the weekend. There is also a night service known as Nightlink and operates after these hours Mon-Sat, and bank holiday Sundays.

Luas

One of the most popular forms of public transport, the Luas is a Light Rail Transit System, essentially a tram service running on separate tracks and roads. There are just two lines, red and green. Both run from the city-centre. Dart

Dart

Dublin Area Rapid Transit (DART) is an electrified train service which runs along the coast of the city from Malahide and Howth in the North, southwards to Greystones, County Wicklow.
