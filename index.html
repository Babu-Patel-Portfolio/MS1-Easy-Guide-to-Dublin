Drink

Dublin is famous for its nightlife and pubs. Whatever the occasion or taste, there will be a pub to suit your needs. Pubs to sit in and have a pint and a chat or traditional Irish food and entertainment. There are far too many to mention, but here are a few to give you an idea of what’s on offer.

Traditional Pubs

The Foggy Dew is a popular bar but always seems a little too loud and a bit packed. Located around Temple Bar.

Doyles is a wonderfully quaint pub, ideal for a quiet pint. Located on College Green.

The Long Stone is just beside Trinity College, full of students and locals. Good for a few pints, though you may struggle to find a seat.

Tourist Pubs

Fitzsimons is a good place to watch the football and have a few drinks. Live music, on most nights. Located on Eustace St in Temple Bar.

The Oliver St. John Gogarty markets itself to tourists as a traditional Irish bar, offering Irish music, food and drink.

The Auld Dubliner offers Traditional Irish Music performances. Say no more. Located at 17 Anglesea Street, Temple Bar.

Modern Pubs

The Odeon used to be a railway station. Opens till late. Moderately priced compared to Temple Bar pubs. Located on Harcourt Street.

Dakota is a sophisticated bar located on South William Street. Great for an afternoon or evening drink.

Cafe en Seine has beautiful décor that nicely complements the beautiful crowd. Cool place to hang out. Located on Dawson Street.

Travel

Dublin city is served by a number of travel options providing good transport links within the city and to the rest of the country, though not always good value. Again it's worth checking out seasonal offers, particularly when travelling by train during the summer months.

Bus

Dublin Bus is the main provider of a regular public bus service within the city. A reduced service operates at the weekend. There is also a night service known as Nightlink and operates after these hours Mon-Sat, and bank holiday Sundays.

Luas

One of the most popular forms of public transport, the Luas is a Light Rail Transit System, essentially a tram service running on separate tracks and roads. There are just two lines, red and green. Both run from the city-centre. Dart

Dart

Dublin Area Rapid Transit (DART) is an electrified train service which runs along the coast of the city from Malahide and Howth in the North, southwards to Greystones, County Wicklow.
